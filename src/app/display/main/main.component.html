A few months ago, your son went for a walk with your three Shi-Tzu dogs, with all three dogs tied to a rolling leash that allows movement for dogs with relative ease. When your son passed by John Doe's house one of the dogs walked toward the gate of his house and started sniffing around. At that moment your son ran to free the dog from the hound's teeth, and when he managed to release him from the grip, he saw that the dog was bleeding heavily from his wounds. Upon his return home, you drove to the veterinary hospital to get care of the injured dog. After several hours of treatments and emergency surgery performed on your dog, the doctors managed to save his life, but the dog lost one of his eyes and a major scar remained on his face.

When you returned from the veterinary hospital with the dog you decided to call John Doe and talk to him about the incident. John Doe answered the phone but apparently was afraid of the possibility of recording the call and using it against him in case the incident comes to court, so he spoke coldly and refused to apologize. His answer was "the gate of the garden was locked".
